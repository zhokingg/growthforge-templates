our story

From a single
country office
in Oxfordshire

Terra began in 1892 when Arthur Ashburton, a gentleman farmer turned land agent, opened a small office on Burford high street with nothing more than a ledger, a map of the Cotswolds, and an unfailing ear for the kind of house that could not be found by asking. Within a decade he had placed forty families into farmhouses and rectories across three counties.

His son Edward carried the firm through two wars, quietly rescuing unloved manors from decline and matching them with new custodians. By the 1960s, Terra was known in certain drawing rooms as the agency you spoke to first, long before a property was ever advertised.

Today, under the fourth generation, the ethos has not shifted. We remain a small team. We walk every acre before we speak of it. And we believe, as Arthur did, that the right house finds the right family only when both are known well.

our philosophy

What it means
to buy a country home

✦

A country estate is not purchased in haste, and it is not sold as one might sell a flat in town. These are the four principles that have guided our work for more than a century.

est.
1892
i.
A considered approach
We take the long view. A family may visit three or four times across a year before deciding, and we think that is exactly as it should be. Country houses reveal themselves slowly, in changing light and weather.
ii.
Intimate knowledge of the land
Our specialists are countrymen and countrywomen first. We know the rivers, the shooting, the soils, and the neighbours. We know which villages still hold a May fair and which lanes flood in February.
iii.
Protecting what we inherit
Listed buildings, ancient woodland, and walled gardens do not belong to any one owner. They are held in trust for those who come after. We will not sell a house to a buyer who does not understand that.
iv.
A lifetime relationship
When the papers are signed our work does not end. We introduce craftsmen, gardeners, estate managers, and land agents. Many of our families have been with us for three generations or more.
