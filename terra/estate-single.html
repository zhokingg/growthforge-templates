the property

A home of considered beauty

Chartwell Manor sits in a gentle fold of Oxfordshire countryside, approached by a quarter-mile avenue of pleached limes. The house itself is an early Georgian composition of warm honey stone, set above a south-facing terrace that catches the sun from breakfast until dusk.

Inside, the rooms retain their original proportions and character — stone flagged floors in the hall, oak panelling in the library, and a drawing room with French doors opening onto the parterre. The principal bedrooms look over the park, and a discreet east wing offers staff or guest accommodation.

Beyond the gardens lie 180 acres of pasture, arable land, and mature woodland, together with a chalk stream carrying wild brown trout. It is a house that has been loved by the same family for three generations, and now seeks another.

i.
Principal reception rooms
A flagstone great hall, south-facing drawing room, panelled library with working fireplace, and a formal dining room seating twenty.
ii.
Walled kitchen garden
A productive acre within eighteenth-century brick walls, with two restored lean-to glasshouses, a potting shed, and espaliered fruit trees.
iii.
Formal parterre gardens
Eighteenth-century box parterre designed in the French manner, with a central lily pond and clipped yew topiary flanking the terrace steps.
iv.
Stable yard & coach house
A cobbled yard with five loose boxes, a tack room, and a former coach house offering scope for conversion, subject to the usual consents.
v.
Chalk stream & woodland
Half a mile of single-bank fishing on a classic Oxfordshire chalk stream, together with forty acres of mixed broadleaf woodland.
