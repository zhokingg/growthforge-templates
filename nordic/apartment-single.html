A space for living

The Atelier occupies the south-facing corner of the fourth floor, with three full-height windows that draw light across a wide plank of white oak from early morning until dusk. The living room holds a quiet geometry: eleven-foot ceilings, a single lime-washed wall, and nothing in the way.

The kitchen was made in Denmark — solid oak cabinetry, honed limestone, and a small brass tap. Miele appliances disappear behind matched panels. A private balcony opens off the main bedroom, facing a row of ginkgo trees that turn gold in October.

Every surface in the Atelier was chosen to age. The floors will darken. The brass will soften. The walls will take on the faint texture of a home that has been lived in well.
